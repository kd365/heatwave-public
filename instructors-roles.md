# The Infrastructure & Board: Instructor and TA Roles

To simulate a high-pressure, real-world DevOps environment, your instructors will not be acting as traditional teachers this week. Instead, they will occupy specific roles within the **Department of Integrated Logistics (DIL)** ecosystem.

---

## 1. The Senior Architects (Technical Consultants)

- **The Interaction:** You can "book" an instructor for a 15-minute high-level architectural review.
- **The Rule:** They will not debug your code. They will ask questions about your logic, your fail-safes, and your integration points to ensure your "firm" isn't building a house of cards.

---

## 2. The Procurement Officers (Scope Control)

- **The Interaction:** If your firm is over-extended or missing milestones, the Procurement Officer may step in to "de-scope" your project.
- **The Result:** They may force you to pivot from a complex feature to something simple or may require you to remove a feature all-together to ensure you deliver a functional MVP by Saturday.

---

## 3. The Regulatory Board (The "Wrenches")

- **The Interaction:** The Board may issue a **Market Volatility Alert** in Slack.
- **The Result:** These are universal technical or business hurdles (e.g., a new security requirement or a budget cut) that every firm must incorporate into their workflow, with a return time of ASAP.

---

## 4. The Security Auditors (Compliance)

- **The Interaction:** Instructors will perform random **"Desk Audits"** of your AWS Console and GitHub Repos.
- **The Result:** If they find security risks, such as hardcoded credentials, "Allow All" IAM policies, or unencrypted data, your contract status will be moved to **"At Risk"** until the vulnerability is patched.

---

## 5. The Acquisition Board (Saturday, Day 4 PM)

- **The Interaction:** On Saturday, the instructors become the **"Shark."**
- **The Result:** They will judge your final demo based on technical stability, your ability to handle the "Wrenches," and the professional delivery of your Agentic Workflow.
